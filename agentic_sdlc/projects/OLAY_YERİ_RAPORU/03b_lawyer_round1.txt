The prosecutor's review, while highlighting some points for clarification, fundamentally misunderstands and misinterprets critical aspects of the evidence. **ANALYSIS OF PROSECUTOR'S ACCUSATIONS:**

The prosecutor focuses on three main areas: the identity of the person leaving the building, the origin of the weapon, and a perceived contradiction between "controlled attack" and "defense scratches." These points are either based on assumptions not present in the file or represent a failure to connect the dots as presented.

**IDENTIFICATION OF WEAKNESSES IN PROSECUTOR'S ARGUMENTS:**

The prosecutor's argument suffers from a lack of critical thinking and an overemphasis on minor details that do not negate the core evidence.

1.  **"The Second Exit" Ambiguity:** The prosecutor states, "If only one person left at 22:03, who else could have left, or how did they leave undetected?" This question is based on a false premise. The file states, "22:03 → 1 kişi hızlı şekilde binadan çıkıyor" and "22:06 → sistemde ikinci çıkış yok." This does *not* mean a second person *left* and was unrecorded. It means only *one* person was recorded leaving the building *at all* after the meeting. The implication is that E1 was the last person to leave, and no one else from the meeting left later. The prosecutor is creating a problem where none exists by assuming a second person *must* have left.
2.  **Opportunity:** The prosecutor questions the timing based on the 22:03 exit. However, the death occurred between 21:50 and 22:10. E1 leaving at 22:03 places them squarely within the time of death window, having been present in the office with Selim Karaca. The presence of E1's DNA on the victim's fingernails is direct evidence of E1's physical involvement *during* the attack, regardless of the exact departure time.
3.  **Weapon Origin:** The prosecutor states, "It's not explicitly stated that this knife belongs to the office kitchen or was brought by E1." This is irrelevant. The crucial fact is that E1's fingerprint is on the knife, and it was used to kill Selim Karaca. Whether it was an office knife or brought by E1 is secondary to E1's direct physical connection to the murder weapon. The presence of E1's DNA on the coffee cup and Selim's fingernails, along with E1's fingerprint on the knife, establishes E1's handling of these items.
4.  **"Saldırı ani ve kontrollüdür" vs. Defense Scratches:** The prosecutor claims these are contradictory. This is a misinterpretation. "Ani ve kontrollü" refers to the *nature of the fatal blow* – a swift, precise stab to the heart, indicating the attacker knew what they were doing and acted decisively. The "savunma çizikleri" (defense scratches) on the victim's wrist indicate the victim's reaction *during* the sudden attack, attempting to ward off the blow or disarm the attacker. This is entirely consistent with a swift, decisive attack that the victim tried to resist. It does not imply a prolonged, uncontrolled struggle.

The sequence of events – meeting, confrontation, attack – culminating in E1 being the only person recorded leaving the building after the estimated time of death (22:03 exit, within 21:50-22:10 window) points solely to E1. The absence of a second recorded exit simply means no one *else* left after E1.
